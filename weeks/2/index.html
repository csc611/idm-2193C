Way of Seeing Response

Brief Synopsis of Ways of Seeing

Created by John Berger in 1972, Ways of Seeing is a video series consisting of four sections, which all bring forth the idea that art is subjective; this prompts us to look at art, and the world around us, in a different point of view.

Concepts and Analysis

Out of the four episodes, the first sparked my interest. The aforementioned primarily discusses philosopher Walter Benjamin’s text The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction, in which Benjamin defines that each work of art has its own distinctive aura. This “aura” can be experienced when the particular art piece is in its original, intended form and in its intended “space and time”. However, this aura can be altered when massively reproduced. The art piece is now stripped of its original form/material and is in an entirely different place, to be seen by people with, most likely, completely different cultural backgrounds. Berger uses this to exemplify his argument that mass reproduction of art may nullify or lessen the sense of “worth” that a piece of art may have because it is so easily accessible to the public.

Thoughts

This specific topic intrigued me because I love photography and graphic design and I find that whenever I look back on old photos that I took or designs I made, I feel a certain nostalgia because I have memories of that piece. However, due to the change and the passage of time, they may hold a new meaning for me. I also find it interesting that no matter who looks at the photos I take, every single individual will have a different perspective and emotional responses.
